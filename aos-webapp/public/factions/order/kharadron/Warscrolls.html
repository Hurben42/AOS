      32.2 Objective Control
      In the deployment phase after all Deployment Phase abilities have been used and at the end of each turn, follow this sequence for each objective in an order chosen by the active player:
      1. Starting with the active player, each player determines the control score of each of their units that is contesting that objective. A units control score is the combined Control characteristics of all the models in that unit that are contesting the objective. Some abilities modify a units control score, but it cannot be reduced to less than 1.
      2. Each player adds up the control scores of all of their units contesting that objective. This is their army control score for that objective.
      3. The players compare their army control scores for that objective. If one players score is higher, that player gains control of that objective. Once a player gains control of an objective, it remains under their control until their opponent gains control of it.

      Sometimes objective markers get accidentally nudged while you are moving models around. This is perfectly fine - just remember to put them back in their proper positions when determining objective control.
